Travel & Stay

Getting to Shangri-La Boracay

Most guests are flying in from abroad, so plan on two flights — into Manila first, then a short hop to Caticlan, the airport closest to the island. Tell us where you’re starting and we’ll map out the journey and rough travel time.

01
Fly to Manila (MNL)

02
Connect to Caticlan (MPH)

A roughly one-hour Philippine Airlines flight from Manila to Caticlan — the airport closest to the resort, and the only one you’ll need. Please allow at least 4 hours between landing in Manila and this flight to clear immigration, collect your bags, and change terminals.

03
Resort Transfer · Van & Speedboat

Once you clear Caticlan arrivals, the Shangri-La team meets you at their dedicated counter — a short van ride to the jetty, then a private speedboat (about fifteen minutes) around the headland to the cove. All included; simply send the resort your flight details ahead of time.

Most guests are flying in internationally, so plan on two flights. First, fly into Manila (NAIA), the Philippines’ main international gateway. Just send the resort your flight details at least 3 days before arrival.

We’ve planned three days of celebrations, each with its own dress code — there’s a full visual guide with color palettes on the Schedule page. In short:

Day 3 · Ceremony & Reception (Jan 29): Formal tropical attire in earthy, sun-drenched tones — sage, olive, green, muted yellow, warm tan, and brown. Ties welcome (black ties are fine). Please avoid ivory and white out of respect for the bride, and kindly don’t wear a black dress or black suit.

January is one of the best times to visit Boracay! It falls within the Amihan (northeast monsoon) season, which typically brings warm, sunny days with refreshing sea breezes. Average temperatures are 26–30°C (79–86°F). There may be occasional light rain, but prolonged storms are rare. The resort has beautiful indoor backup venues for the ceremony in the unlikely event of heavy rain — you’re in great hands.
